Types of Yoga

Traditional Yoga Styles

1. Hatha Yoga: Focuses on physical postures (asanas) and breathing techniques (pranayama).

2. Raja Yoga: Emphasizes meditation, self-reflection, and spiritual growth.
3. Bhakti Yoga: Focuses on devotion, love, and spiritual connection.
4. Karma Yoga: Emphasizes selfless service and action.
5. Jnana Yoga: Focuses on knowledge, wisdom, and intellectual understanding.

Modern Yoga Styles

1. Ashtanga Yoga: Fast-paced, physically demanding style.
2. Vinyasa Yoga: Focuses on flowing movements and breathing.
3. Iyengar Yoga: Emphasizes precise alignment and props.
4. Kundalini Yoga: Focuses on energy, breathwork, and spiritual awakening.
5. Restorative Yoga: Uses props for relaxation and rejuvenation.
6. Yin Yoga: Targets deep tissue relaxation and flexibility.
7. Power Yoga: Physically demanding and fast-paced.
8. Sivananda Yoga: Classical style emphasizing Five Points of Yoga.
9. Kripalu Yoga: Focuses on compassionate, mindful movement.
10. Anusara Yoga: Emphasizes positive thinking and self-acceptance.

Specialized Yoga Styles

1. Prenatal Yoga: For expectant mothers.
2. Yoga Nidra: Deep relaxation and guided meditation.
3. Chair Yoga: Modified yoga for seniors or mobility limitations.
4. Aerial Yoga: Uses aerial equipment for support.
5. SUP Yoga: Stand-up paddleboard yoga.
6. Hot Yoga: Practiced in heated environments.
7. Yin-Yang Yoga: Balances opposing energies.
8. Chakra Yoga: Focuses on energy centers and spiritual growth.
